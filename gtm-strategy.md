# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy: Assignment Builder

## 1. Executive Summary
**Product:** Assignment Builder — an AI-powered SaaS for teachers to instantly generate, manage, and export question papers.
**Objective:** Transition from beta testing with a small group of early adopters to a wider rollout across regional schools, targeting 100+ active educators within 6 months.
**Core Value:** Turn a 2-hour formatting and question-hunting chore into a 5-minute delightful experience.

---

## 2. Target Market & User Personas

### Primary Persona: High School Teachers (The Champion)
* **Pain Points:** Overworked, spending evenings formatting Word documents, struggling to find good questions, and meticulously trying to calculate test marks.
* **Motivation:** Wants to reclaim personal time while still delivering high-quality, standardized assessments to students.
* **Buying Power:** Zero to low (decision maker for free tools, but not school budgets).

### Secondary Persona: Head of Department / Principal (The Buyer)
* **Pain Points:** Inconsistent exam quality across different classes, lack of a centralized curriculum repository, teachers experiencing burnout.
* **Motivation:** Standardization, efficiency, and school prestige.
* **Buying Power:** High (approves software budgets).

---

## 3. Positioning & Messaging
* **The Hook:** "Stop formatting Word documents. Build your next exam in 5 minutes."
* **Value Pillars:**
  1. *Speed:* AI-generated questions and drag-and-drop UI.
  2. *Quality:* Centralized, peer-reviewed question bank.
  3. *Professionalism:* Export to beautiful, print-ready PDFs instantly.

---

## 4. Acquisition Channels (The Rollout Plan)

### Phase 1: The "Do Things That Don't Scale" Pilot (Months 1-2)
* **Tactic:** Direct, white-glove onboarding.
* **Execution:** Take the teachers who already agreed to use it. Ask them for their past papers in PDF/Word format. Manually digitize their questions into the database so their first login is a "Wow" moment. 
* **Goal:** Create 5 absolute super-fans who will advocate for the product in their teacher staff rooms.

### Phase 2: Word of Mouth & Internal Virality (Months 3-4)
* **Tactic:** Referral loops within the same school.
* **Execution:** Include a subtle watermark on the bottom of free-tier exported PDFs: *"Generated in 5 minutes with AssignmentBuilder.app"*. When a teacher shares a great assignment with a colleague or prints it out, the tool advertises itself.
* **Goal:** Expand organically from 1 teacher to the entire department (e.g., all math teachers in the school).

### Phase 3: Community & Content-Led Growth (Months 5-6)
* **Tactic:** SEO and Teacher Communities.
* **Execution:**
  * Post in specialized Facebook Groups / Reddit communities (e.g., r/Teachers). Offer free access to the "AI Question Generator."
  * Publish SEO-optimized printable test papers (e.g., "10th Grade Algebra Midterm PDF Template") that directly link to the app to download or edit.

---

## 5. Onboarding & Retention Strategy
Acquiring a teacher is easy; keeping them is hard.
* **Frictionless Sign-up:** Google OAuth integration. No credit card required.
* **The "Aha!" Moment:** Must happen within the first 3 minutes of login. Provide a pre-built "Demo Exam" they can export immediately to see the final PDF quality.
* **Retention Loop:** Weekly automated email reminders containing "3 new AI-generated questions for [Their Subject]" to prompt them to log back in and create a weekend assignment.

---

## 6. Success Metrics & KPIs (The PM Analytics)
* **Acquisition:** Number of signups via referral vs. direct traffic.
* **Activation Rate:** % of new signups who successfully export their first PDF assignment.
* **Engagement:** Average number of questions added to the bank per week; AI-generation requests per user.
* **Retention:** % of teachers who create 2 or more assignments within a 30-day window.
